Circuit Chase Competition: Rules and Regulations

About: Line Follow Robot

The line-following robot competition highlights the cutting edge of robotics and precision engineering, attracting teams and enthusiasts worldwide to showcase the capabilities of autonomous robotics. Set in an atmosphere of innovation and excitement, this event provides a platform for engineers, designers, and programmers to demonstrate their expertise in robotics. Participants from a range of academic institutions, research labs, and industries come together to test their robots on intricate tracks and challenging paths, pushing the limits of precision, agility, and adaptability in autonomous navigation.

Game Rules

1.Robot Design: Build a manually operated robot to follow a pre-defined path without interference.

2.Auto-Stabilization: No auto-stabilization or external support hardware allowed.

3.No Alterations: No robot adjustments between rounds.

4.Unique Robots Only: Each team must use its own robot; sharing between teams is not allowed.

5.Damage Liability: Organizers are not responsible for damages within the arena.

Robot Specifications

• Dimensions: Max 30x30 cm.

• Sensors: Line-tracking sensors only.

• Speed: Max speed of 1 meter per second.

• Power: Battery max 300g, voltage limit 12V.

• Construction: No sharp edges or metal parts that could damage the track.

Judgment Criteria

1. Precision: Ability to stay on the line during turns.

2. Speed: Time to complete the track as a primary metric.

3. Penalty Points: Penalties for line exit or repositioning.

4. Completion: Track completion without penalties earns bonus points.

Team Guidelines

• Team Size: Up to 4 members.

• Collaboration: Teams may be cross-institutional.

• Representatives: Only two members allowed in the arena vicinity at once.
